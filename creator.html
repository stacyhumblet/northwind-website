What Your Analytics Can't Tell You - Yet

Platform analytics are built to show you what happened inside their walls. Here's what they'll never show you on their own.

🔀

You're checking three platforms to get one picture

Shopify. Then Instagram. Then TikTok. Separately.

▾

Right now you open Shopify to check sales, switch to Instagram to check content, then open TikTok to check views - and there's no single place where all three talk to each other. You're manually connecting dots that should be automatic. Our dashboards pull all three into one view so you can see the full picture at once, not in three browser tabs.

🎯

You have no way to set a goal and track whether you'll hit it

Platforms show what happened. Not whether you're on pace.

▾

Native analytics tell you your revenue last month was $18,000. But is that good? Are you ahead of target? Behind? On pace for your quarterly goal? No platform will tell you that - because they don't know your targets. Our dashboards include a Goals & Targets layer where you set the number, and we show you exactly where you stand against it, updated automatically.

📈

You can't see where you're headed - only where you've been

No platform shows you a forecast. They only report history.

▾

Every platform analytics tool is a rearview mirror. They show you what already happened. None of them tell you: "at your current pace, you'll hit $X by end of month" or "your follower growth is trending toward Y by Q3." We build trend-based forecasting directly into your dashboards so you can see what's coming - not just what already passed.

🔗

You don't know which post actually drove that sale

IG shows reach. Shopify shows revenue. Nobody connects them.

▾

Instagram will tell you a Reel got 40,000 views. Shopify will tell you you had a great sales day. But neither platform will tell you those two things are related - or by how much. We build the attribution layer that maps social content to downstream purchases, so you know exactly which posts are driving revenue and which ones are just getting views.

📅

You can't annotate your own data

Platforms don't know about your promotions, collabs, or price changes.

▾

You ran a 20% off sale. You posted a collab with a big creator. You changed your pricing. Platforms have no idea any of that happened - so when you look at a spike in your data three months later, you have no context for why it happened. Our annotation layer lets you mark events directly on your charts so your data always tells the full story, not just the numbers.

💸

Revenue looks great - but you don't know your actual profit

Shopify knows your sales. It doesn't know your costs.

▾

Shopify reports revenue and orders - but it doesn't know your cost of goods, your ad spend, your shipping overhead, or your software subscriptions. We can connect your business expense data alongside your Shopify revenue to give you a true net margin view. Because a $30,000 revenue month with $28,000 in costs is a very different story than the analytics dashboard suggests.
